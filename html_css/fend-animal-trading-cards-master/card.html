Florida Panther

Fewer than 230 Florida Panthers are estimated to live in the wilds of South Florida today.

  • Threats: Habitat loss, fragmentation, and degradation
  • Diet: Feral hog, white-tailed deer, raccoon and armadillo
  • Habitat: Hardwood hammocks and pinelands
  • Causes of Mortality: Automobile collisions and territorial aggression between panthers

It was formerly considered critically endangered by the IUCN, but it has not been listed since 2008. Recovery efforts are currently underway in Florida to conserve the state's remaining population of native panthers.
